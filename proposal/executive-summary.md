Meridian Components' operations team is running a dashboard that was delivered incomplete. The Reports module has known defects that haven't been resolved. The Restocking capability your VP of Operations requested doesn't exist yet. And your IT team has correctly identified that without automated test coverage, no change to the system is safe to approve.

These are not separate problems. They are the same problem: a previous engagement that ended before the work was done.

We are proposing to finish it — and to leave the system in a state where your internal team can maintain and extend it without needing another vendor.

**Our approach has four phases, in priority order:**

1. **Unblock IT first.** We will establish automated browser test coverage for the Reports module — the area of highest defect risk — before we touch anything else. This gives your IT team a safety net and the confidence to approve subsequent changes.

2. **Fix Reports.** We will conduct a structured discovery sprint to catalog every defect in the Reports module (we understand Meridian's tracker will be shared upon award), then resolve each one. Filter behavior, internationalization gaps, data inconsistencies — all of it.

3. **Build Restocking.** We will deliver the new Restocking view: purchase order recommendations based on current stock levels, demand forecast, and an operator-supplied budget ceiling. This is the capability your operations team has been waiting for.

4. **Document the architecture.** We will produce a current-state architecture overview suitable for handoff to Meridian IT — a real document, not the thin notes the previous vendor left.

**On the desirable items** (UI modernization, extended i18n, dark mode): we have scoped these as optional add-ons. UI modernization in particular is meaningful effort — adopting a modern component library is a real rebuild, not a polish pass — and we want to be honest about that rather than bury it in a line item. We will price these separately so Meridian can choose.

We have read the previous vendor's handoff notes. We know what we're inheriting. We are not going to tell you it's in better shape than it is — but we are confident we can bring it to where it should have been at the end of the last engagement, and beyond.
